Endemic, Vulnerable, Endangered and Extinct Species

The species which no longer exist anywhere on the earth are called extinct animals. These have died out completely. A species become extinct when the last living member of that species dies, e.g. dodo, dinosaurs, iris deer, etc.

Animals whose numbers are diminishing to a level that they might face extinction are called endangered animals. These are in the verge of vanishing from the earth and if proper actions are not taken, they may become extinct, e.g. Asiatic Lion, White Tiger, Lion-Tailed Macaque, etc.

Endemic Species are the species of plants and animals which are found exclusively in a particular area. They are not naturally found anywhere else, e.g. sal and wild mangoes are the endemic species of plants in the Pachmarhi Biosphere Reseve. Bison, Indian Giant Squirrel and flying squirrel are the endemic fauna of that region.

Vulnerable species are the species of the animals which are in the verge of becoming endangered and henceforth they are protected in high security by the Government of India.
